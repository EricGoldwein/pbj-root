# Non-Nursing Staff in PBJ

This page describes non-nursing job categories in the [Payroll-Based Journal (PBJ)](/pbjpedia/overview) system and explains how non-nursing staff hours are reported.

Although PBJ is often discussed in the context of nurse staffing, the system also collects detailed information on non-nursing roles that are essential to nursing home operations. PBJ distinguishes between employees and contract staff and reports hours worked for each job category on each day. Understanding non-nursing staffing patterns can shed light on administrative capacity, clinical oversight, and ancillary services.

The [PBJ policy manual](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/quality-initiatives-patient-assessment-instruments/nursinghomequalityinits/downloads/pbj-policy-manual-final-v24.pdf) assigns standardized job codes to non-nursing positions. Each record in the [public use files](https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/dataset/4pq5-n9py) contains total hours, employee hours, and contract hours for every category.

| Job Code | Role | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Administrator** | Facility management (administrator and assistant administrator). PBJ may exclude administrator hours when five or more administrators are reported on four or more days in a quarter. |
| 2 | **Medical Director** | Physician responsible for resident care policies per 483.75(i). Hours may be low when services are billed outside the facility payroll. |
| 3 | **Other Physician** | Attending or supervisory physician other than the medical director. |
| 4 | **Physician Assistant** | PA providing services under physician supervision. |
| 13 | **Nurse Practitioner** | NP licensed to diagnose and treat (reported in non-nursing PUF when not performing nursing-category hours). |
| 14 | **Clinical Nurse Specialist** | RN with specialized graduate training (same reporting note as NP). |
| 15 | **Pharmacist** | Consultant or staff pharmacist for drug regimen review and controlled-substance systems. |
| 16 | **Dietitian** | Registered or qualified dietitian for dietary programs. |
| 17 | **Paid Feeding Assistant** | Paid staff who feed residents without complicated feeding needs (per 483.35(h)(2)). |
| 18 | **Occupational Therapist** | Licensed OT (includes OTs spending under 50% of time as activities staff). |
| 19 | **Occupational Therapy Assistant** | Licensed/certified OT assistant. |
| 20 | **Occupational Therapy Aide** | OT aide under therapist supervision. |
| 21 | **Physical Therapist** | Licensed PT. |
| 22 | **Physical Therapy Assistant** | Licensed PT assistant. |
| 23 | **Physical Therapy Aide** | PT aide under therapist supervision. |
| 24 | **Respiratory Therapist** | Licensed respiratory therapist. |
| 25 | **Respiratory Therapy Technician** | RT technician under direction of RT/physician. |
| 26 | **Speech/Language Pathologist** | Licensed SLP. |

| 28 | **Qualified Activities Professional** | Activities professional per 483.15(f)(2). |

| 30 | **Qualified Social Worker** | Licensed or qualified social worker per CMS definition. |
| 31 | **Other Social Worker** | Other social work staff (not volunteers). |

Nursing job codes **5–12** (director of nursing through medication aide) are covered on [PBJ Metrics](/pbjpedia/metrics).

## Significance

Non-nursing staff make up a substantial share of nursing home operations. Administrators ensure regulatory compliance; physicians and therapists oversee clinical and rehabilitation services; dietary and environmental staff support daily resident needs. Contract hours are reported separately, so analysts can measure reliance on agency staff by role and state. Hours for physicians and non-physician practitioners are often underreported when services are billed to Medicare or other payers rather than paid on facility payroll. See [Data Limitations](/pbjpedia/data-limitations).
