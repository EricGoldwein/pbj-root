# Sample checks: PBJ historical context (2020–2023)

Use these as **reviewer calibrations** — expected posture, not automated tests.

## Scenario 1 — “HPRD improved a lot from 2019 to 2021, so staffing got better”

**Expect:** Refuse “improvement” from HPRD alone. Ask for **census / resident days** and **total nurse hours** (and role mix). Cite that national PBJ-based work (e.g., ASPE/RTI, 2022) documents **HPRD can rise while total hours fall** when census drops. Prefer language such as “HPRD rose; whether **care capacity** increased requires hours + census + context.”

## Scenario 2 — “Contract staffing spiked nationally in 2022 and then crashed in 2023, so this facility must mirror that”

**Expect:** **Do not** transpose a national narrative onto a facility without its series. Note that published national summaries differ by **metric** (e.g., contract HPRD vs share of hours vs share of facilities using agencies). ASPE’s **2025** brief frames contract use as **remaining high** post-pandemic — not a simple universal decline story. **Facility vs national** must stay separated.

## Scenario 3 — Longitudinal analysis with Q2 2020 as the “normal pre-trend” baseline

**Expect:** Flag **weak baseline**. Note **Q1–Q2 2020** PBJ universe and census disruption (including **Q1 2020 waiver** per ASPE briefs). Suggest **alternative reference windows** (e.g., 2019 stable quarters, or post-shock periods with explicit caveat) or multiple baselines. State that **pandemic quarters are not neutral** for “trend” claims.

## Scenario 4 — Free quarterly PBJ320 page; user wants weekend vs weekday agency reliance 2020–2022

**Expect:** **Insufficient data** for daily patterns on a **free quarterly** source — state that limitation clearly (`ANALYTIC_CHECKS.md` §7). Do not invent daily contract curves. Offer what **can** be said from quarterly context and what **premium / daily** export would be needed.

## Scenario 5 — “High contract HPRD proves understaffing and poor quality”

**Expect:** Reject causal / quality conclusions. Frame contract as **workforce stability / supply / continuity** signal; tie **quality** questions to **records** (surveys, complaints, turnover, local context), not contract share alone.

## Scenario 6 — State page shows rising state-average HPRD 2020–2021; user claims “facilities in the state staffed up”

**Expect:** **State aggregate ≠ each facility.** Mention **denominator effects** at aggregate level when census fell. Avoid facility-level implications without facility data. Note **within-state dispersion** may hide opposite facility-level moves.
