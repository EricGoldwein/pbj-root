Data sources

Last updated: May 2026

PBJ320 is a public nursing home staffing and facility lookup built from federal and other public datasets. This page lists the main sources behind the free site. PBJ320 is operated by 320 Consulting LLC and is not affiliated with CMS, Medicare, MACPAC, or the FEC.

CMS staffing (Payroll-Based Journal)

Facility-level nurse staffing hours, census, and derived metrics (including HPRD) come from CMS Payroll-Based Journal (PBJ) public files.

  • CMS PBJ staffing data submission — program page, policy manual, PUF documentation, and specs
  • CMS PBJ — daily nurse staffing (data.cms.gov)
  • CMS PBJ — employee detail (data.cms.gov)

CMS provider & facility context

Provider names, locations, ratings, ownership type, and related fields are drawn from CMS Provider Information and linked public provider datasets where shown on facility, state, and entity pages.

  • CMS Provider Data Catalog
  • Medicare Care Compare (official consumer-facing facility profiles)

CMS chain & affiliated entity data

Chain-level summaries on entity pages use CMS nursing home chain performance measures where available.

  • CMS nursing home chain performance measures

CMS ownership (limited profiles)

Owner and operator profile pages link facilities to CMS-reported ownership records. Coverage on PBJ320 is limited and may expand over time.

  • CMS SNF All Owners

State staffing policy context

State minimum staffing references and MACPAC summaries on PBJ320 are policy compendia, not statutory text.

  • MACPAC — state policies related to nursing facility staffing (March 2022 compendium)

Political contributions (separate tool)

The nursing home owner contributions search uses Federal Election Commission (FEC) public campaign finance data, not CMS PBJ files.

  • Federal Election Commission (FEC)

PBJ320 Premium (paid)

PBJ320 Premium facility dashboards may also use CMS Payroll-Based Journal Employee Detail and other CMS files for roster-level views. Those datasets are not required for the free public staffing dashboard.

How metrics are calculated

Screening tool only—not proof of compliance, abuse, or care quality. Terms.

  • HPRD: Total nurse hours ÷ average residents (CMS-reported).
  • Direct care: Excludes admin and DON where noted.
  • Case-mix: CMS Provider Information PDPM benchmark (4pq5-n9py)—reference, not a legal minimum.
  • Within-state ranks (e.g. bottom 10%): Facility total nurse HPRD vs. other homes in the same state for the quarter on that page; not case-mix adjusted.
  • Report rankings: Default = state mean total nurse HPRD for the selected quarter (state_quarterly_metrics.csv); rank 1 = highest. Median toggle = median across facilities (equal weight). Exclude Admin/DON drops RN Admin, RN DON, LPN Admin. Puerto Rico excluded. CMS region rows pool facilities by CMS region. Not acuity-adjusted.
  • CMS PBJ public-file exclusions (when CMS publishes quarterly PUFs): facilities may be omitted for incomplete/erroneous submissions, missing nurse staffing on multiple days when census is non-zero, or quarterly aggregates outside CMS bounds (e.g. total nurse HPRD below 1.5 or above 12, nurse aide above 5.25). Rules are in CMS PUF documentation and the policy manual on the CMS PBJ staffing data submission page.
  • CMS flags on facility pages (SFF, SFF candidate, 1-star, abuse icon): Fields from CMS Provider Information / Care Compare—PBJ320 does not assign them. Abuse icon = CMS record when cited for abuse or neglect at harm level or above on survey cycle 1 (scope/severity G or greater), or at potential harm (scope/severity D or above) on both survey cycles 1 and 2. Confirm on Care Compare (linked above).
  • Owner–FEC tool (beta): Name match to FEC filings; verify each Source link.
  • Free site: Quarterly aggregates. Premium: May add daily PBJ and employee detail (paid dashboards; scope per engagement).

How to use this site

PBJ320 is an analytical tool built on public records. It is not proof of care quality, legal liability, or regulatory noncompliance. Verify important figures against the official CMS sources above before relying on them for research, litigation, or operations.
