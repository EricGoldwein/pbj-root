# PBJ historical context: 2020–2023 / COVID era (PBJ320)

Use this reference when **any reviewed period overlaps January 1, 2020 through December 31, 2023**, or when trends are anchored to quarters in that window. It is a **review discipline** module: verify claims against CMS PBJ-based literature and official reporting context, and avoid unsupported “improvement” narratives.

## Standing rules (language and inference)

1. **Never** treat a change in **total nurse HPRD alone** as “staffing improved,” “better staffed,” or proof of higher caregiving capacity. HPRD is **hours per resident-day**; the denominator (resident census / resident days) moved sharply for many facilities and nationally during COVID.
2. **Always** pair HPRD with **census / resident-day trajectory** and, when available, **total nurse hours** (or category hours). A rise in HPRD alongside falling census and falling total hours is consistent with a **denominator effect**, not a staffing surge.
3. **Contract / agency hours** — describe as **workforce supply, turnover, and continuity** signals. **Do not** infer poor care, neglect, or quality solely from higher contract share; **do not** claim contract staffing **caused** harm or good outcomes without non-PBJ evidence.
4. **MACPAC / state policy summaries** — orientation only; **not** statutory compliance (see `pbj320_macpac_state_standards.md`).
5. **Separate scales** — national or state **aggregate** patterns do not determine what happened at a **specific facility**; keep facility findings and national context distinct.
6. **Baselines for longitudinal claims** — quarters with **extreme census movement or disaster staffing** (especially **2020**, often **early–mid 2021**) are **poor “neutral baselines”** for long-run trend statements. Prefer comparisons that acknowledge the shock window or use later, more stable reference periods when the question allows.
7. **Source depth** — Free quarterly PBJ320 pages may omit daily contract series, full census history, or total-hour tables visible on premium exports. **Acknowledge missing fields** instead of inferring.

**Preferred phrasing:** “may reflect,” “consistent with,” “warrants checking census and total hours,” “interpret alongside…,” “not sufficient to infer…,” “national summaries suggest… but this facility’s pattern should be read from its own series.”

## Verification notes (national PBJ-based evidence)

### A. Census fell while HPRD could rise (denominator vs true staffing lift)

**Verified (national, PBJ-based, 2019–2020):** ASPE/RTI analyses of **CMS Payroll-Based Journal** show **average daily census declined** month by month in 2020 vs 2019, while **average total daily nurse hours** also fell April–December 2020; **nonetheless total nurse HPRD rose** in many months because resident counts dropped faster than hours in aggregate terms.

- ASPE issue brief (Sept 2022), *Nursing Home Nurse Staff Hours Declined Notably During the COVID-19 Pandemic…* — PDF: https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/30c70f4bb332d44e3e162a9a0b3d1044/nh-nurse-staff-hours-brief.pdf  
- Companion piece on contract reliance (same program): https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/67e96aa09cd62304787c331ea6c2c6a6/nh-reliance-contract-staff-brief.pdf  

**Additional peer context:** Health Affairs analysis (2020) on PBJ data during COVID reported **limited overall change in staffing levels** in a national sample frame — interpret alongside ASPE’s emphasis on **simultaneous** census and hour movement; different endpoints and windows can produce different headline summaries.

- https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/full/10.1377/hlthaff.2020.02351  

**Reviewer takeaway:** A national **pattern of rising HPRD with falling census and falling total hours** is **documented** for 2020 in ASPE’s PBJ briefs. **Do not** auto-translate facility-level HPRD upticks into “they staffed up.”

### B. Contract / agency staffing — timing (national vs facility)

**Verified (2020, national descriptive PBJ work):** ASPE/RTI: **contract nurse staffing HPRD averaged ~24% higher in 2020 than 2019** across months; increases varied by **profit/chain status, star rating, resident mix**, etc. (same contract brief URL as above).

**Post–acute pandemic characterization (national policy summary):** ASPE (Jan 2025) — *Use of Contract Staff in Nursing Homes Remains High After the COVID-19 Pandemic* — landing page: https://aspe.hhs.gov/reports/use-contract-staff-nhs-remains-high-after-covid-19-pandemic — issue brief PDF: https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/0efd4ee9d0d0e9e67c5442141053d71c/contract-staff-nhs-high-after-covid.pdf  

**Academic synthesis:** Health Affairs Forefront (2023) documents growing **agency reliance in direct care** using PBJ-linked analysis (headline national framing; still not a substitute for facility-level review): https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/10.1377/hlthaff.2023.01101  

**Uncertain / do not over-claim:** A simple statement like “**national** contract share **peaked in 2022 and then fell**” is **not** established here at a single authoritative threshold — different papers use different windows, roles, and denominators (**hours share** vs **contract HPRD** vs **facilities-using-agency**). For **2021–2023** nuance, **cite the specific study and metric** or say the **direction is facility- and measure-dependent**.

### C. Data and reporting limitations (interpretive, official)

- **Q1 2020 PBJ submission:** ASPE/RTI briefs note **fewer facilities in some early-2020 months** because CMS **waived** PBJ submission requirements in **Q1 2020** (see appendices in the ASPE PDFs). Treat **Q1 2020** aggregates cautiously.
- **PBJ = facility-submitted administrative data**, not bedside audit — see `pbj320_limitations.md`.
- **MDS-linked census** underpinning resident-day denominators can move with **admissions pauses, discharges, deaths, and length-of-stay mix** — ASPE stakeholder discussion in the nurse-hours brief lists multiple drivers besides mortality alone.

## What you can and cannot infer

| Situation | Reasonable | Not supported from PBJ320 / PBJ alone |
|-----------|------------|--------------------------------------|
| HPRD up, census down | “HPRD may be **mathematically elevated** by census; check total hours.” | “Staffing improved.” |
| Contract HPRD elevated | “**Consistent with** national stress-era workforce patterns; raises **continuity / reliance** questions.” | “Poor care” / “negligence” / causal harm |
| Trend from 2019→2021 | “**Pandemic-era bridge**; baseline choice matters.” | “Return to normal” without defining stable window |
| State aggregate page | “State **context** for facility” | Same as **this facility’s** contract mix without facility data |
