# PBJ320 Limitations Reference

## What PBJ data can help show

- Reported daily and quarterly staffing hours by broad role category
- HPRD and facility-level staffing patterns
- Trends over time, contract mix, day-of-week patterns (Premium/daily views)
- Comparisons across facilities, states, regions, or ownership groups
- CMS case-mix benchmarks and Harrington expected (when shown — see `pbj320_harrington_vs_casemix.md`)
- Potential dates or periods worth further review
- Screening outliers or red-flag days (hypothesis only)

## What PBJ data cannot prove by itself

- What happened on a specific shift or unit assignment
- Whether a specific resident received care from a specific worker
- Whether staffing caused an injury, infection, fall, elopement, or death
- Whether a facility violated a legal standard or was negligent
- Whether submitted data is complete, accurate, or matches payroll/timecards
- Whether staffing was clinically sufficient for every resident need
- PASS/FAIL on state minimums from a MACPAC reference band alone (`references/pbj320_macpac_state_standards.md`)

## Data quality and verification

- Staffing and census are **facility-reported to CMS**. PBJ320 displays and analyzes CMS releases; it does **not** independently audit payroll, time clocks, or clinical records.
- Employee Detail (EIN roster) is facility-reported; coverage typically from ~**Q2 2020** forward.
- **Pandemic-era quarters:** For longitudinal claims, treat **2020–**early **2021** especially as a potential **shock window** (census, hours, contract mix, Q1 2020 PBJ reporting caveats) — see `references/pbj320_historical_context.md`.
- Provider Information and PBJ quarters are **aligned by PBJ320 methodology** but remain distinct CMS products — note the quarter on each metric.
- **No invented numbers:** If a metric is missing from the page or export, say it is not shown; do not fabricate decimals or ranks.

## AI-specific caution

- AI summaries are not verified evidence. Users should cite CMS datasets and the PBJ320 page/export, not the AI paraphrase alone.
- Do not treat case-mix or Harrington benchmarks as legal minimums.
- Do not treat screening flags, volatility labels, or percentiles as findings of fault.

## Records to seek for incident-specific work

Shift schedules, assignment sheets, timecards, payroll, agency invoices, daily census, care plans, acuity records, incident reports, complaints, citations, survey findings, staffing meeting notes, and ownership/management change records.
