# Lean visual examples (PBJ320 review practice)

Concrete patterns to exercise the rule in `pbj320_visual_requirement.md`. Use **real numbers from attachments only**.

## Multi-quarter total HPRD trend

| Quarter | Total nurse HPRD |
|---------|------------------|
| 2023-Q2 | 3.80 |
| 2023-Q3 | 3.72 |
| 2023-Q4 | 3.61 |

Takeaway example: Total nurse staffing inches down over three quarters visible here.

## HPRD rises while census falls (2020–2021 caveat)

Tiny table **or** two-line chart: paired **census** and **HPRD** for quarters where **both** are in the CSV. Takeaway ties to analytic gate (“HPRD can rise without more staff hours if census drops”).

## Contract share spike then decline

One series of **contract %** quarters (if column present). Takeaway stays descriptive; flag continuity limits, not fault.

## Facility reported HPRD vs CMS case-mix reference (same quarters)

Bars or paired columns **only if** both facility **and** case-mix reference values exist in the paste. Language: screening comparison — not sufficiency.

## Insufficient numeric data

Material is narrative-only or missing quarter rows → **complete** review with explicit “cannot build a numeric visual — need [trend CSV / census column / peer percentiles …].”

## Attorney mode, quarterly-only

Same visual as analyst **if** it supports strongest finding — **takeaway** names timing limits (“quarter averages; not shift-level or incident-aligned without daily extracts”).

## Family vs researcher, identical numbers

Same table/plot allowable; family: shortest labels (“care hours per resident day,” avoid unexplained abbreviations); researcher: denominators and confounds in prose beside the graphic.
