New York Nursing Homes Fell Below the State’s 3.50-Hour Staffing Minimum on Nearly Half of Days in 2025

57.1% of facility-days statewide reported staffing below the 3.50 HPRD standard; 78.4% on weekends. Quarterly statutory-style mapping (3.50 / 2.20 / 1.10) is in the full report. All 596 homes are searchable.

BROOKLYN, N.Y. — June 3, 2026

For immediate release

Press release body

Media contact: Eric Goldwein, MPH · eric@320insight.com · (929) 804-4996 WhatsApp / text

BROOKLYN, N.Y. — PBJ320 analyzed 216,134 daily staffing records from 596 New York nursing homes in 2025 and found that 123,428 facility-days — 57.1% — reported staffing below New York’s 3.50 HPRD direct-care standard. On weekends, 78.4% of facility-days fell below 3.50 HPRD. These daily counts describe reported staffing levels, not NY DOH quarterly compliance determinations.

Why this matters

Nursing home staffing is often reported as quarterly or annual averages. This analysis uses federal Payroll-Based Journal (PBJ) data — one record per nursing home per calendar day — to describe day-to-day variation against New York’s 3.50 hours per resident day standard.

The headline metric is the share of facility-days below the standard, not a single statewide average. A home can run near or above 3.50 on paper for the year while still logging many days under the line — especially on weekends. These patterns matter for workforce planning, enforcement, resident advocacy, and how policymakers, families, and the public read New York staffing data.

Each facility-day is one home on one calendar day with census greater than zero. Hours per resident day (HPRD) is nurse staffing hours divided by MDS census; below standard means strictly under 3.50. Charts, county and ownership slices, the 596-home table, quarterly statutory-style rollups, and full methodology are in the interactive report.

Key findings

Statewide

57.1% of facility-days reported staffing below 3.50 HPRD (123,428 of 216,134).

Weekends

78.4% of Saturday–Sunday facility-days were below 3.50 HPRD (48,302 of 61,586). In NYC, weekends were 83.4% (14,162 of 16,978). Sunday was the highest day of the week (80.6%); Wednesday the lowest (42.8%).

Counties

Among high-volume counties, the Bronx had the highest share — 69.3% of facility-days below 3.50 HPRD. Queens was 58.1%. Erie and Monroe were lower among large upstate counties (36.9% and 31.3%).

Ownership

For-profit homes ran higher than nonprofit and government operators (3.50 component): NYC for-profit 67.5%, statewide for-profit 65.1%; statewide nonprofit 42.0%, government 23.8% (NYC government 6.0%).

596 nursing homes

The full report lists every home with its share of days below 3.50 HPRD. 58 homes (9.7%) were below the standard on every day in 2025; 158 homes on at least 90% of days.

Quarterly statutory-style mapping

New York determines staffing compliance quarterly. Among 2,369 facility-quarters analyzed, 59.7% averaged below 3.50 total HPRD and 65.9% missed at least one mapped floor (2.20 CNA-side / 1.10 licensed nurse). 284 facilities were below 3.50 in all four quarters. Descriptive PBJ mapping only—not NY DOH enforcement determinations.

Methodology snapshot

How the numbers were calculated

The analysis uses CMS PBJ daily nurse staffing files for CY2025 Q1–Q4, limited to New York facilities with census > 0. Hours per resident day (HPRD) is nursing hours divided by MDS census. Ownership and county are assigned from CMS Provider Info snapshots aligned to each PBJ quarter where available. Days exactly at 3.50 counted as meeting the threshold; days strictly below counted as below. Default total nursing HPRD includes RN DON/admin and LPN admin; the full report includes a sensitivity view excluding admin/DON.

As secondary context, the statewide CY2025 mean was 3.58 hours per resident day (total nursing) and 3.43 direct care — averages can sit above the minimum while many individual days still fall short.

County maps, day-of-week and ownership charts, the 596-home facility table, role-floor detail, and limitations are in the full PBJ320 report (methodology).

Important notes

PBJ is self-reported federal staffing data, not NY DOH enforcement records or quarterly DOH compliance determinations.

PBJ is daily, not shift-level.

Default total nursing HPRD includes RN DON/admin and LPN admin; the report includes a sensitivity toggle excluding admin/DON.
