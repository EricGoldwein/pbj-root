What is PBJ?

PBJ (Payroll-Based Journal) is the federal system that tracks staffing in U.S. nursing homes. Medicare- and Medicaid-certified facilities must report who worked, what they did, and how many hours—using payroll records—each quarter. The data is public and covers more than 15,000 nursing homes.

Why does it matter?

Staffing levels are one of the strongest predictors of nursing home quality. More staff per resident generally means more time for care.

What is HPRD?

HPRD (hours per resident day) is the standard metric. It answers: On average, how many hours of nursing staff time did each resident get per day? For example, 4.0 HPRD means each resident had the equivalent of 4 hours of nursing care per day. Higher numbers usually mean more staffing; benchmarks often cite around 4.1 HPRD as a recommended minimum for direct care.

Key positions in PBJ

PBJ tracks several nursing roles:

  • RN (Registered Nurse) — Registered nurses; includes administrative and direct-care hours.
  • LPN/LVN — Licensed practical or vocational nurses.
  • CNA — Certified nurse aides; hands-on daily care.
  • Director of nursing (DON) — Manages nursing operations; hours count in total nurse staffing.

PBJ also records non-nursing roles (administrators, medical directors, therapists, dietary, housekeeping, etc.) and whether hours are from facility employees or contract staff.

What PBJ doesn't tell you

PBJ reports paid staffing hours only. It doesn't include wages, volunteer time, resident complexity, or overall care quality. It shows staffing levels — not care outcomes — and should be interpreted alongside other information about a facility.
