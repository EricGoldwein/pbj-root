Staff categories

  • Total Staff: All nursing staff including RNs, LPNs, CNAs, RN/LPN administrators, and DON.
  • Direct Staff: Direct patient care (excludes administrators and DON).
  • Total RN: RNs, RN administrators, and RN DON.
  • Direct RN: Excludes RN Admin and RN DON.
  • LPN: Direct care LPNs and LPN administrators.
  • Nurse Aides: CNAs, nurse aide trainees, and medication aides.
  • Contract Staff: Temporary or agency staff (PBJ fields marked with “_ctr” suffix).

Calculations

  • HPRD: Total hours ÷ census.
  • Contract staff %: (Contract hours ÷ total hours) × 100.
  • Averages: Weighted by census when applicable.
  • Rounding: Standard half-up rounding for displayed metrics.
  • % Case-Mix HPRD: (Reported HPRD ÷ CMS case-mix expected HPRD) × 100, from Provider Information. Below 100% means reported staffing under the CMS-reported acuity benchmark for that quarter. See the Five-Star / Care Compare Technical Users’ Guide (PDF) and Nursing Home Quality Initiatives. Dataset: Nursing Home Provider Information.
  • Non-direct staff: RN Admin/DON and LPN Admin hours per resident day.

Harrington expected staffing

Expected staffing adjusts for resident acuity using CMS Case-Mix Index (CMI):

  • Total Expected HPRD: 3.48 + ((CMI − 0.62) / (3.84 − 0.62))0.715 × (7.68 − 3.48)
  • RN Expected HPRD: 0.55 + ((CMI − 0.62) / (3.84 − 0.62))0.974 × (2.39 − 0.55)
  • Nurse aide expected HPRD: 2.45 + ((CMI − 0.62) / (3.84 − 0.62))0.236 × (3.6 − 2.45)
  • % of expected: 100 × (reported HPRD ÷ Harrington expected HPRD), rounded; CSV “diff” columns = reported − expected.

Citation: Nursing Home Guide to Adjusting Nurse Staffing for Resident Case-Mix (Harrington et al., Journal of the American Geriatrics Society).

Note: Direct-care metrics from PBJ exclude Admin/DON as described above.

CMS context: Care Compare / Five-Star Technical Users’ Guide (PDF) · Nursing Home Quality Initiatives.

Screening statistics

  • Z-score: (value − mean) ÷ standard deviation.
  • Outliers (screening): |Z| ≥ 2.0 (high), |Z| ≥ 3.0 (extreme).
  • Percentile: Approximate rank vs. historical values in the view.
  • Holidays: U.S. federal holidays as defined for dashboard logic.

Employee Detail (EIN) and daily PBJ

When Premium includes Employee Detail, roster and trends use this crosswalk. CMS releases typically start ~Q2 2020; earlier PBJ quarters may have no EIN rows.

Flow: PBJ bucket (underlined) → day roster → employee quarter trend.

PBJ bucket to EIN codes
  • Hrs_RN → 7
  • Total RN → 5, 6, 7 · Hrs_RNadmin → 6 · Hrs_RNDON → 5
  • Hrs_LPN → 9 · Total LPN → 8, 9 · Hrs_LPNadmin → 8
  • Hrs_CNA (CNA line only) → 10
  • Total nurse aide (Hrs_CNA + Hrs_NAtrn + Hrs_MedAide) → 10, 11, 12
Roster job codes (EMPLEE_JOB_CD_ID)

1 Administrator · 5 RN DON · 6 RN Admin · 7 RN · 8 LPN Admin · 9 LPN/LVN · 10 CNA · 11 aide in training · 12 med aide

Role position numbers (#1, #2, …): Within each job code, employees are ordered by first work day in the loaded Employee Detail file (then employee ID). Labels are for readability only. Inspection deficiency tags and Form 2567 citations come from a different CMS system and are not tied to these roster numbers.

Roster cross-check: PBJ daily column sum vs. Employee Detail code sum for that date (warning threshold about ±2.5 hours or ~3%). Direct RN: Hrs_RN vs. code 7. Total RN: Hrs_RN + Hrs_RNadmin + Hrs_RNDON vs. codes 5+6+7.

Data sources

Staffing metrics come from CMS Payroll-Based Journal (2017 onward). Provider Information (4pq5-n9py) supplies ratings, ownership, case-mix, and related fields. Rows are aligned to PBJ quarters using reported total staffing where both sources overlap; other fields may refresh on a different cadence—see PBJ ↔ Provider Information matching.

Employee-detail data: CMS PBJ Employee Detail.
