Appendix

Data Sources and Methodology

This report is based on data from the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Payroll-Based Journal (PBJ) system. The PBJ system requires nursing homes to submit daily staffing data, including:

  • Total nursing staff hours (RN, LPN, CNA, and other nursing staff)
  • Contract staff hours
  • Daily resident census

Hours Per Resident Day (HPRD) is calculated by dividing total nursing hours by total resident days for each quarter. This metric provides a standardized measure of staffing levels that accounts for variations in census.

State averages are calculated using weighted averages (total hours divided by total resident days) for all facilities in the state of NY.

Provider information matching

PBJ data is quarterly; hours and census are rolled from daily submissions, so quarter-level totals are accurate.

Provider info (CMS Provider Information files) is published on a rolling basis and has both a quarter and a processing_date. How we use it in this report:

  • Red flags (SFF, 1-star, abuse icon, Admin TO, ownership): we include rows whose processing_date falls within the report’s date range. The quarter we display is from the file or derived from that date—it is an estimate of when the flag applied, not an exact incident date.
  • Case-mix: when the report has specific quarters, we match provider info rows by quarter (row’s quarter must be in the report’s quarter list). Otherwise we filter by processing_date within the report range.

New York State Staffing Requirements

New York State Staffing Requirements: 3.56 HPRD (Source: N.Y. Public Health Law § 2803-d | MACPAC)

State Standard: 3.56 HPRD

New York requires nursing homes to maintain minimum staffing levels to ensure adequate care for residents. The state has established a minimum of 3.56 hours per resident day (HPRD) of total nursing staff.

Compliance Analysis

Requirement HPRD Description
Minimum Total Staffing 3.56 HPRD New York requires nursing homes to maintain a minimum of 3.56 hours per resident day of total nursing staff (RN, LPN, and nurse aides—CNA, Med Aide, NA Trn—combined). This includes both direct care staff and administrative staff.
RN Staffing Minimum Required Facilities must maintain adequate RN staffing to meet resident care needs. RN hours include both direct care RNs and RN administrators/DON.
CNA Staffing Minimum Required Certified Nurse Aide staffing must be sufficient to provide direct care services to all residents.

Enforcement: Facilities that fail to meet New York minimum staffing requirements may be subject to penalties, fines, and enforcement actions by New York State Department of Health.
