Verify facility and period

  • CCN. Confirm the Certification Number (CCN) in the header matches pleadings, Form 2567, and Care Compare.
  • Dates. Match the audit window to your operative period. PBJ is work-date based; quarters follow CMS reporting. Tie labels to methodology in court filings.
  • Provenance. Note data vintage and dashboard deployment date before circulating outside the team (when shown).

Section-by-section walkthrough

One reasonable order—reorder to fit your theory.

  1. Summary. Scale (census, hours, HPRD), recent movement, headline indicators.
  2. Compliance. Federal minimums and state rules where loaded—legal conclusions remain yours.
  3. Benchmarks. Peer and geography context; document comparator logic if challenged.
  4. Trends. Longitudinal staffing, contract mix, day-of-week patterns, pre/post windows around reported corporate events.
  5. Employee tracker. When present: position and employer rollups from CMS employee-detail releases—self-reported to CMS.
  6. Daily PBJ. Work-date drill-down; capture filters and CCN on exhibits.
  7. Red flags. Screening views for unusual days—hypothesis generators, not proof of substandard care.
  8. Methodology. On-report definitions; cross-check with the standalone Methods page when drafting declarations.

Examples

Example
Fall or wound date tied to staffing

Filter to the incident week in Daily PBJ; compare direct HPRD and contract share to the prior 30 work days. Export or screenshot with the date range, CCN, and category definitions from Methods.

Example
Change of ownership or operator

Use Trends and Provider Information context to bracket the quarter; run pre/post windows that match your allegations, then reconcile to entity identifiers and CMS ownership flags as described on-report.

Example
Expert report or 30(b)(6) outline

Work top-down: Summary and Benchmarks for narrative, Compliance and Trends for timeline charts, Daily PBJ for specific dates referenced in the record. Cite CMS datasets (see Methods) as the underlying sources.

Discovery and deposition angles

  • Quarter tie-out. Align PBJ rollups to payroll or cost reports by quarter and staffing category; note material mismatches for follow-up.
  • Contract staffing. Track agency or contract hours if relevant to notice, adequacy, or staffing stability theories.
  • Adjacent facilities. PBJ320 entity or chain views can add context beyond one CCN.

Exhibits and citations

  • State URL, date accessed, CCN, and active filters on declarations or exhibit lists.
  • Prefer citing CMS primary datasets in filings; use the dashboard as a structured view with Methods for definitions.

Limitations

Staffing and census are facility-reported to CMS; PBJ320 does not audit against payroll or time clocks.
